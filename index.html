What is an Avocado?

The Oxford Dictionary definition of an avocado is: "a pear-shaped fruit with a rough leathery skin, smooth oily edible flesh, and a large stone". Avocados are native to Central America and widely cultivated elsewhere. In order to tell if an avocado is ripe, there are a few details to note. Ripe avocados will have a skin color that is dark green to nearly black. The consistency and texture of avocados are also key. Ripe avocados will also have skin with a bumpy texture and will have a consistency to where you are able to gently squeeze the avocado.

Can You Grow Your Own Avocado Tree?

The answer is yes! Once you eat the avocado and remove the pit, there are multiple methods that will successfully lead to the pit sprouting a root. All methods involve first cleaning the pit by washing it with warm water and then typically peeling any excess skin from the pit. From there, there is what is known as the toothpick method, the paper towel method, and directly planting the pit into soil. The method that worked best when I attempted to grow an avocado tree was the paper towel method where you wrap a damp paper towel onto the pit and then place the wrapped pit in a Ziploc bag and seal it with no excess air in the bag. After a few weeks of the bag being in a location that remains the same temperature, conveniently in a pantry corner, you will spot a root sprouting from a now cracked avocado seed. From there, pot the avocado root under soil and wait for the sapling to grow!

EXTRA INFORMATION

TRUE OR FALSE?
You can freeze avocados.

True! Some people assume that freezing an avocado will damage it. The only change that will occur is logical. Also, frozen avocados should be thawed at room temperature for about an hour. They are best used in multi-ingredient dishes, such as guacamole.

FRUIT?VEGGIE?
Its a fruit!

An avocado is a fruit. A fruit develops from the flower of a plant. Fruits contain seeds, while vegetables can consist of roots, stems and leaves. 

FUN FACT
There are different types of avocados.

Since avocados are a large berry fruits containing a single seed, there are over 500 varieties. These vary in fruit size, texture, shape, and maturity rate.
